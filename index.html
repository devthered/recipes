Classic British Scones

Mischa Lewis-Norelle

  • 285 g (2 cups) flour
  • 40 g (3 tbsp) sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 70 g (5 tbsp) cold unsalted butter
  • 120 g (1 cup) heavy cream (or buttermilk or coconut milk)

Preheat oven to 425˚F.

Combine dry ingredients and butter with food processor or fingertips until mixture resembles coarse sand. Stir in any mix-ins. Then put the mixture in the freezer for 15 minutes, or overnight for easy scones tomorrow morning.

Stir in cream until a dough begins to form. Then transfer to a floured work surface and knead together until the dough just comes together in a rough, slightly sticky ball. Gently pat dough into a ¾-inch-think circle, and cut into 8 wedges. Bake until tops are light brown, 12-15 minutes. Let cool on rack for 10 minutes before serving with butter (or clotted cream if available) and jam.

Variations:
Jalapeno Cheddar: omit sugar. Add only 3 tbsp butter along with 4 oz sharp cheddar cheese. Add ½ cup jalapenos.
Cranberry Orange: add ½ cup cranberries, the zest of an orange, and 2 tbsp orange liqueur.
Ginger, Lemon, Mint: add ½ cup crystallized ginger, the zest of a lemon, 2 tbsp lemon juice or extract, and ¼ cup chopped mint.

Pasta Aglio e Olio

Babish

  • 1/2 head garlic, separated and peeled
  • 1 bunch parsley, rinsed
  • 1/2 cup good quality olive oil
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 lb dry linguine
  • 1/2 lemon

Heavily salt a large pot of water, and bring to a boil. Cook pasta to al dente while completing the steps below.

Slice the garlic cloves thinly, and set aside. Pick the parsley leaves from their stems, and finely chop. Add olive oil to a large sauté pan, and heat over medium flame until shimmering. Add sliced garlic, stirring constantly, until garlic is barely toasted. Add the red pepper flake and remove from heat.

Add the pasta, drained, with about 1/4 cup reserved pasta cooking water. Add lemon juice and parsley, and toss to combine. Season for salt and pepper, and serve.
